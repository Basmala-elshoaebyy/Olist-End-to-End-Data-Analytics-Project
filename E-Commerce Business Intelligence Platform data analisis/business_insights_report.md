### Olist E-Commerce Business Intelligence Report

# Executive Summary

This project analyzed Olist's Brazilian e-commerce marketplace data to evaluate revenue performance, customer behavior, product performance, operational efficiency, and customer satisfaction.

The analysis combined multiple datasets including orders, customers, products, reviews, and logistics information. Data cleaning, feature engineering, SQL analysis, and business intelligence techniques were used to generate actionable insights for management decision-making.

Key findings indicate strong revenue concentration in a few states, significant growth throughout 2017 and 2018, high-performing product categories, and a clear relationship between delivery speed and customer satisfaction.

### 1- How Has Revenue Changed Over Time?
# 1. Revenue Trend Analysis

Revenue demonstrated substantial growth throughout the analyzed period.

Revenue increased from approximately BRL 137,188 in January 2017 to more than BRL 1.15 million by April 2018, representing significant marketplace expansion.

The strongest revenue periods occurred between November 2017 and August 2018. November 2017 generated approximately BRL 1.18 million in revenue, likely influenced by seasonal shopping events and holiday promotions.

Overall, the business experienced consistent growth, indicating successful customer acquisition and increased marketplace activity.

### 2- Which States Generate the Most Revenue?
# 2. Revenue by State

Revenue is heavily concentrated in a small number of states.

Top Revenue States:

* São Paulo (SP) – BRL 5.92 million
* Rio de Janeiro (RJ) – BRL 2.13 million
* Minas Gerais (MG) – BRL 1.86 million
* Rio Grande do Sul (RS) – BRL 885,827
* Paraná (PR) – BRL 800,935

São Paulo alone contributes a disproportionately large share of total revenue, making it the company's most important market.

Management should prioritize customer retention, inventory allocation, and marketing investments in these high-performing regions while exploring growth opportunities in lower-performing states.

### 3- Which Customer Locations Generate the Most Orders?
### 3- Customer Location Analysis

Order volume closely follows revenue distribution.

Top States by Order Volume:

* São Paulo (SP) – 41,746 orders
* Rio de Janeiro (RJ) – 12,852 orders
* Minas Gerais (MG) – 11,635 orders
* Paraná (PR) – 5,045 orders

The concentration of orders in these states indicates that demand is geographically clustered.

Future expansion efforts should investigate the characteristics of customers in these regions and replicate successful strategies in emerging markets.


### 4- What Is the Repeat Customer Rate?
# 4- Customer Retention Analysis

The repeat customer rate was approximately 3.12%.

This indicates that the vast majority of customers placed only a single order during the analyzed period.

While customer acquisition appears successful, customer retention remains a significant opportunity for improvement.

Potential strategies include loyalty programs, personalized promotions, email marketing campaigns, and improved post-purchase engagement.


### 5- Which Product Categories Generate the Most Revenue?
# 5- Product Revenue Analysis

Top Revenue-Generating Categories:

* Health & Beauty – BRL 1.44 million
* Watches & Gifts – BRL 1.31 million
* Bed Bath & Table – BRL 1.24 million
* Sports & Leisure – BRL 1.16 million
* Computers & Accessories – BRL 1.06 million

These categories represent the primary revenue drivers of the marketplace.

Management should prioritize supplier relationships, inventory planning, and marketing campaigns within these categories to maximize future revenue growth.


### 6- Which Categories Receive Poor Reviews?
#  6- Product Satisfaction Analysis

Lowest Rated Categories:

* Security & Services – 2.50 average rating
* Diapers & Hygiene – 3.26 average rating
* Office Furniture – 3.49 average rating
* Home Comfort 2 – 3.63 average rating
* Fashion Male Clothing – 3.64 average rating

These categories consistently receive below-average customer ratings.

Management should investigate product quality, supplier performance, packaging quality, and delivery conditions for these categories to improve customer satisfaction.


### 7- What Is the Average Delivery Time?
# 7- Delivery Performance

The average delivery time across completed orders was 12.09 days.

While this performance is reasonable for nationwide logistics operations, reducing delivery times could significantly improve customer experience and review scores.

Delivery efficiency should remain a key operational performance indicator.

### 8. Which Regions Experience Delayed Deliveries?
# 8- Delivery Delay Analysis

States with the Longest Average Delivery Times:

* Roraima (RR) – 27.83 days
* Amapá (AP) – 27.75 days
* Amazonas (AM) – 25.96 days
* Alagoas (AL) – 23.99 days
* Pará (PA) – 23.30 days

These regions experience delivery times that are significantly above the company average.

The findings suggest logistical challenges related to geographic distance, transportation infrastructure, or warehouse distribution networks.

Operational improvements in these regions may improve customer satisfaction and reduce negative reviews.

### 9-What Factors Contribute to Low Review Scores?
# 9- Review Score Analysis

A strong relationship exists between delivery speed and customer satisfaction.

Average Delivery Time by Review Score:

* 1-Star Reviews: 19.12 days
* 2-Star Reviews: 15.34 days
* 3-Star Reviews: 13.58 days
* 4-Star Reviews: 11.75 days
* 5-Star Reviews: 10.19 days

Customers who waited longer for delivery consistently gave lower ratings.

This finding suggests that delivery performance is one of the most influential factors affecting customer satisfaction.

Reducing delivery times is likely to improve review scores and overall customer experience.


### Final Recommendations Section


1. Continue investing in high-performing categories such as Health & Beauty, Watches & Gifts, and Bed Bath & Table.

2. Prioritize customer retention initiatives, as repeat purchase behavior is currently very low.

3. Expand logistics capabilities in northern states where delivery delays are most severe.

4. Improve operational efficiency to reduce average delivery times and increase customer satisfaction.

5. Investigate low-rated categories to identify product quality, supplier, or logistics issues.

6. Leverage successful strategies from São Paulo, Rio de Janeiro, and Minas Gerais to stimulate growth in emerging regions.

7. Monitor delivery performance as a key business metric due to its direct impact on customer reviews.
